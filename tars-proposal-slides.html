The Challenges

Incident triage is still a largely manual process

Alerts fire, but triage is manual — teams scramble to correlate alerts with logs, metrics, and recent deployments by hand
High MTTR — root cause discovery is the bottleneck — most incident time is spent finding the problem, not fixing it
Siloed observability — logs, metrics, and traces scattered across platforms with no unified view
Manual, repetitive triage — engineers repeat the same diagnostic steps for recurring issue patterns
Knowledge siloed with key SMEs — expertise walks out the door with attrition, runbooks go stale
No standardized incident response — quality depends on who’s on call, not a repeatable process
Troubleshooting requires platform expertise — only a few engineers know where to look across complex distributed systems
